AI and Human Collaboration

Artificial intelligence continues to reshape how humans work, communicate, and make decisions. Collaboration between people and intelligent systems is rapidly becoming the default mode in many industries.

Interface-Level Decision Nudging

User interfaces powered by AI often influence decisions subtly, guiding people toward recommended actions without explicit instruction.

Managing Automation Bias

Automation bias occurs when users rely too heavily on AI suggestions, especially when the models appear confident or authoritative.

Consequences of Over-Trust

Excessive trust in AI systems can lead to overlooked risks, particularly in areas like aviation, healthcare, and autonomous vehicle navigation.

Explainability Challenges

Even when explanations are provided, they can be incomplete or simplified, leaving users with a false sense of clarity about how decisions are made.

Hidden Model Limitations

Many AI models rely on hidden assumptions about data quality and environmental stability. When these assumptions break, performance may silently degrade.

Re-Evaluating AI Oversight

Oversight frameworks must evolve in parallel with AI capabilities. Human review, transparency tools, and robust testing environments are essential to avoid blind reliance on automated systems.
